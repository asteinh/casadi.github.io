Nonlinear programming

Nonlinear programs (NLPs), possibly with integer variables (MINLP), can be solved using block structure or general sparsity exploiting sequential quadratic programming (SQP) or interfaces to IPOPT/BONMIN, BlockSQP, WORHP, KNITRO and SNOPT. Quadratic programs (QPs), possibly with integer variables (MIQP), can be solved using a primal-dual active-set method [3] or interfaces to CPLEX, GUROBI, HPMPC, OOQP or qpOASES.
Problem class: $$ \begin{array}{cc} \begin{array}{c} \text{minimize:} \\ x \end{array} & f(x,p) \\ \begin{array}{c} \text{subject to:} \end{array} & \begin{array}{rcl} x_{\textrm{lb}} \le & x & \le x_{\textrm{ub}} \\ g_{\textrm{lb}} \le &g(x,p)& \le g_{\textrm{ub}} \end{array} \end{array} $$

$$ \begin{equation} \begin{array}{cl} \underset{\begin{array}{c}x, y, z\end{array}}{\text{minimize}} \quad & x^2 + 100 \, z^2 \\ \text{subject to} \quad & z+(1-x)^2 - y = 0. \end{array} \end{equation} $$
